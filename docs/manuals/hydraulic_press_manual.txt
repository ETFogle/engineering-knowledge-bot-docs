Hydraulic Press Operation and Maintenance Manual

Section 1 – Overview
The hydraulic press is used for forming, stamping, and pressing operations. It operates using high-pressure hydraulic fluid to generate force.

Section 2 – Safety Precautions
- Never place hands inside the press area during operation.
- Always verify E-Stop functionality before each shift.
- Lockout/Tagout must be applied before maintenance.

Section 3 – Operating Parameters
- Nominal pressure: 2000 psi
- Maximum pressure: 2500 psi
- Stroke length: 250 mm
- Cycle time: 8 seconds

Section 4 – Common Faults
Fault: Press will not build pressure
Possible Causes:
- Low hydraulic fluid level
- Relief valve stuck open
- Pump coupling sheared

Fault: Press cycles slowly
Possible Causes:
- Clogged return filter
- Air in hydraulic lines
- Worn pump

Section 5 – Preventive Maintenance
Daily:
- Check fluid level
- Inspect for visible leaks

Weekly:
- Clean filter screens
- Inspect hoses for abrasion

Quarterly:
- Replace hydraulic fluid
- Inspect cylinder seals

Section 6 – Troubleshooting Flowchart
1. Verify power and E-Stop status.
2. Check hydraulic fluid level.
3. Inspect pump motor overload.
4. Measure system pressure at test port.
